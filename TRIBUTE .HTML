Biography

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, better known as APJ Abdul Kalam, was a renowned Indian scientist and the 11th President of India. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, and passed away on July 27, 2015. Dr. Kalam played a crucial role in the development of India's space and missile programs and is widely regarded as the "Missile Man of India." He was a great visionary and an inspiration to millions of people around the world..

Dr. Kalam received numerous awards and honors for his contributions to science, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award. He was also a prolific author and wrote several books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds," which continue to inspire generations.
